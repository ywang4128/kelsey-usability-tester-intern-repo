# Learning Goals

## What do you hope to learn from this internship?
- deepen my understanding of designing and testing digital products
- how real user behavior can differ from assumptions in design.
- how people with AuDHD experience challenges, and how to anticipate issues for neurodivergent users.
- how to effectively identify and communicate usability issues.
- how to think of non-ideal behaviors or unexpected usage pattern systematically
- Australian workplace culture

## What skills are you most excited to develop?
I'm most excited to develop my ability of evaluating and designing features of the app and the platform, and turning my ideas into constructive feedback.  
I also want to sharpen my accessibility awareness and learn how to anticipate challenges for neurodivergent users.

## What do you think you can contribute to the team?
I can contribute a combination of user empathy, critical thinking, and hands-on experience in product operations. I've previously worked on software and platform product design iteration, still learning but I believe i can bring fresh perspectives to usability testing.

## What is one area you feel less confident in that you’d like to improve?
I’d like to improve in testing for neurodivergent-specific issues and to document and explain those problems more clearly and systematically in English.

## What does success in this internship look like for you?
Delivering clear bug reports and actional plan that can really solve problems, making Focus Bear more intuitive, accessible, and supportive for neurodivergent users.  
It would also mean growing my expertise in usability testing and accessibility, being a trusted advocate for real users, and seeing my feedback translated into meaningful improvements in the product experience.
