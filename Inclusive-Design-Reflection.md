# Working with Vulnerable Populations
## Who are considered vulnerable populations, and what challenges might they face in digital spaces?
Neurodivergent people (ADHD, autism, dyslexia), elderly, low-literacy groups, and rural users struggle with complex interfaces, digital divides, and overstimulation in apps.
Challenges: Limited access, skill gaps, harassment, and exclusion from services.

## What ethical considerations are important when designing or working with neurodivergent individuals? (Hint: Avoid overwhelming UX, respect sensory needs, ensure clear communication.)
Prioritize autonomy (user control) and inclusion—consult neurodivergent users early.
Avoid neurotypical assumptions; test for real harm like anxiety from rigid designs.

## How can you make interactions and content more accessible for people with ADHD or Autism? (e.g., simple language, predictable navigation, reducing cognitive load)
ADHD/Autism: Chunked content, mute animations, focus modes, keyboard nav.
Simple language, predictable flows, high contrast, personalization

## How can we support neurodivergent team members in a professional setting? (Hint: Be clear in communication, respect different working styles, and allow flexibility.)
we can support them by giving clear instructions, flexible schedules, quiet spaces, and individualized adjustments.

## How can you adjust your communication style to be more inclusive of neurodivergent users and teammates?
Use direct language, visuals, response time buffers, avoid idioms.

## What are some common UX or communication pitfalls that might make Focus Bear less accessible or supportive?
Common Pitfalls in Focus Bear:
Non-intuitive navigation - Unclear icons, inconsistent button placement across mobile/web, hidden settings
Sensory overload - Flashing notifications, auto-playing audio, rapid animations overwhelm ADHD/autism users
Cognitive overload - Dense text blocks

## What is one practical change you can make in your work to better support vulnerable populations?
Add a mandatory "Neurodivergent Accessibility Checklist" to every Focus Bear bug report I submit.
e.g.
☐ No auto-playing audio/notifications  
☐ No flashing/strobing elements  
☐ Screen has ≤6 interactive items  
☐ Clear error messages (no "oops" text) 
etc.

details could include something like disables all animations/notifications, shows 3 menu items max per screen, etc.
