# How to Be a Member of a Scrum Team #24

## What are the key roles in a Scrum team, and where does QA fit in?
the main scrum rioles are product owner, scrum master, and developers. QA is usually part of the development team, even if not a developer.

## What happens in a Sprint Planning, Daily Standup, Sprint Review, and Retrospective?
sprint planning: the team agrees on what to deliver in the next sprint and how to do it. QA helps clarify AC and what needs to be tested.
daily standup: each member shares what they did yesterday, what they will d today, and any blockers. 
sprint review: the team shows finished work to stakeholders.
sprint retrospective: the team reflects on what went well and what to improve. QA can suggest ways tp reduce bugs and testing delays.

## How does QA help the team maintain quality throughout the sprint?
QA keeps quality high by:
- checking stories as they are completed, not wait until the end.
- writing test cases
- giving fast feedback so developers can fix issues early

## What is shift-left testing, and why is early involvement of QA important?
shift-left testing means moving testing earlier in the development process. Instead of waiting until developers finish coding, QA join when requirements are still being discussed.   
Early involvement helps catch unclear or risky requirement sooner, prevent bugs from being built into app, reduce last-minute testing rushes.

## How do testers collaborate with developers, product managers, and designers in Scrum?
developers: by reviewing stories, pairing on complex features, and helping with test automation.
PM/PO: by clarifying AC and user needs
designers: by checking that the UI matches both design and real-word usability

## How should QA prepare for a Sprint Planning meeting?
before sprint planning, QA should review the backlog items that might be picked for the sprint, think about what needs to be tested, and ask questions about acceptance criteria, test data, and any risks. 

## What information should testers share in a Daily Standup?
in daily standup, QA can share what testing was done yesterday, what will be tested today, and any blockers or risks. This helps the team see QA progress, adjust priorities, and support each other if testing is falling behind.

## If a feature is about to be released, but QA finds a critical bug, what should happen next?
If QA finds a critical bugs just before a release, the team should log the bug clearly with steps, screenshots, and impact. Let the product owner decide whether to fix the bug and delay the release slightly, or ship with the bug and address it in the next sprint, communicate the decision to stakeholders and update release notes if needed.
